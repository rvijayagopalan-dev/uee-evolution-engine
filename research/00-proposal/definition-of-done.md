# Definition of Done — PhD Completion Contract

*What completes the thesis. The finish line, on record and protected from scope
creep. Read with [`doctoral-proposal.md`](./doctoral-proposal.md),
[`../phd-roadmap.md`](../phd-roadmap.md), and
[`../vision-and-scope.md`](../vision-and-scope.md).*

> **Governing principle:** a PhD is completed by **resolving** its falsifiable claims,
> not by **confirming** them. A clean "no" finishes the thesis as well as a "yes."

---

## The one-line bar

> An **original, falsifiable contribution to knowledge**, demonstrated rigorously and
> **defended in a viva**.

The contribution: *a validated, domain-invariant instrument for adaptive capability,
and evidence on whether it transfers and predicts* — **evidence**, not proof that it does.

## Completion contract

| # | Condition | Done when… | Negative result still completes it? |
|---|-----------|------------|:---:|
| 1 | **Gap established** (Study 1) | Systematic review shows the *cross-domain + validated + predictive* cell is empty — or the project pivoted because it wasn't | n/a |
| 2 | **Instrument validated** (Study 2) | 11-level rubric/survey shows **reliability (κ ≥ 0.7)** and **construct validity** | No — must succeed (else nothing is measurable) |
| 3 | **Transfer resolved** (Study 3 / H1) | Structure applied to ≥3 unrelated domains with a clear **yes/no** on unmodified transfer | ✅ "No" (H0) is a finding |
| 4 | **Prediction resolved** (Study 4 / H2) | Maturity tested as predictor of adaptation outcomes vs. baseline — clear result **either way** | ✅ "No" is a finding |
| 5 | **Synthesis** | Four studies integrated into a coherent thesis with stated **contribution, limitations, generalizability** | n/a |
| 6 | **Dissemination** | Typically **2–3 peer-reviewed papers** (programme-dependent) | n/a |
| 7 | **Examination** | Thesis submitted, **viva passed**, corrections completed | n/a |

When 1–7 hold, the PhD is **done** — regardless of whether H1/H2 were positive.

## Minimum viable thesis (fallback if time/data constrain)

Still defensible at reduced scope:
**Study 1 (gap) + Study 2 (validated instrument) + a 2-domain transfer test with an
honest result + partial/inconclusive predictive validity.**

Contribution then: *"the first validated, domain-invariant instrument for adaptive
capability, with initial transfer evidence."* Narrower, but original and examinable.

## NOT completion conditions (scope discipline)

Tying completion to any of these makes the thesis **unfinishable**:

- ❌ Building the "universal evolution engine" (scope 3)
- ❌ Finding the formal invariant / FEP fusion (scope 2)
- ❌ Answering the universal-language-of-intelligence question (scope 4)
- ❌ The 1,000-year design, EEI/ERP-SIM, cosmic synthesis

The PhD **closes a bounded sub-question and stops.** The *programme* continues — that
is what "eternal" means (open-ended programme, finite thesis). See
[`../eternal-research-program-validation.md`](../eternal-research-program-validation.md).

## The real gate

Completion is ultimately a **judgment**, not a checklist: in the viva, two examiners
must be convinced the work is a **genuine, rigorous, original** contribution and that
the candidate can defend its limits. The thesis need not *succeed* — it must be
**sound, original, and honestly bounded.**

---

## One-line summary

> The PhD completes when the four studies resolve their questions (any answer), the
> instrument is validated, the work is published and integrated, and the viva is
> passed — **and explicitly not when the grand vision is achieved.**
