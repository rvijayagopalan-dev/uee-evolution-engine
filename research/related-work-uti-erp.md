## 1. Same central question

The UTI-ERP doc's stated core is:

> *"Can intelligence everywhere be described using one universal language of
> structure, information, and adaptation?"*

This is **verbatim the scope-4 question** in `scope-4-frontier.md`, with the same
three primitives — **structure, information, adaptation**. It is not a different
project; it is another route up the same mountain.

## 5. The sharpest bridge: ERP-X = H1 in silico

The UTI-ERP doc's own ERP-X example —
*"can graph structure learned in ecosystem simulations improve reasoning in economic
simulations?"* — **is the simulated form of our cross-domain transfer hypothesis (H1).**

**Implication:** a small EEI/ERP-SIM prototype can serve as a **cheap in-silico test
of H1**, complementing the field measurement. This is the concrete link between the
two programs.

## 6. Honest caveat — the grandiosity risk

The UTI-ERP doc still carries the overreach its own AI advisor repeatedly warned
against: a 10-paper roadmap ending in a *Science* capstone ("Unified Theory of
Intelligence Across Physical, Biological, and **Cosmological** Systems"), a "universal
intelligence equation," and cosmic-scale CE-ERP. That is the **scope-4-as-assumed
trap** — the same v21→v41 degeneration flagged in our validation doc.

**Cure (the shared scope contract):** let each paper make *one* falsifiable claim;
treat the cosmic capstone as a **conjecture to earn or refute**, never a planned
deliverable.

## 7. Verdict & fusion path

- **Same mountain, same question, independently converged** → mutually corroborating.
- **This track = the disciplined scientific wing** (falsifiable spine + Lakatosian
  governance + real-domain measurement).
- **UTI-ERP = the buildable engineering wing** (AI systems + simulation → a Tier-3
  substrate and an engineering path to testing transfer).
- **Neither has crossed the empirical threshold.**

**Fusion:** use this track as the **falsifiable backbone and scope governance**; use
UTI-ERP's **EEI/ERP-SIM as the AI/simulation substrate** where H1 (= ERP-X) is tested
cheaply in silico. The field study (real domains) and the simulation study (synthetic
domains) then become two independent tests of the *same* invariance claim — far
stronger together than either alone.
