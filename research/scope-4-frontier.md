## The scope-4 question

> **Can intelligence everywhere be described using one universal language of
> structure, information, and adaptation — in a substrate-independent and invariant
> model?**

This sits **above** scope 3 ("a universal science of adaptation") because:
- *intelligence* ⊋ *adaptation* (adds prediction, representation, generality), and
- *one universal language / invariant model* ⊋ *a science* (a generative formalism,
  not just a discipline).

It is a recognized frontier — the **universal theory of intelligence** problem — with
serious, contested, **unclosed** formal contenders. It may also be **unreachable**: a
negative or bounded result (no single substrate-independent invariant language exists)
is a live and respectable outcome, and itself a major contribution.

## The three primitives → existing formal contenders

| Primitive | Candidate formal language | Lead work |
|-----------|---------------------------|-----------|
| **Structure** | Computational mechanics; category theory | Crutchfield; compositional systems theory |
| **Information** | Algorithmic information theory; constructor theory | Kolmogorov/Chaitin; Deutsch & Marletto |
| **Adaptation** | Free Energy Principle; Generalized Darwinism | Friston; Hodgson & Knudsen |
| *(intelligence, as a whole)* | Universal Intelligence / AIXI; IIT (measure) | Legg & Hutter; Tononi |
| *(selection signature)* | Assembly Theory | Cronin & Walker |

The scope-4 task is essentially: **fuse structure + information + adaptation into one
invariant formalism.** No one has done this. AIXI and FEP are the closest existing
partial answers.

## Distance from the current program (honest gap map)

| Axis | This program (scope 1) | Scope-4 question | Gap |
|------|------------------------|------------------|-----|
| Object | Adaptation (adaptive capability) | Intelligence (prediction, representation, generality) | Large |
| Form | Measurement *scale* (11 ordinal, human-rated) | Generative *formal language* (invariant) | Large |
| Substrate | Socio-technical (farms, firms, hospitals) | Everywhere (cells, brains, AIs, markets, physics) | Large |
| Invariance strength | Structural (ordering transfers) | Law-like (conserved quantity / variational principle) | Large |
| Empirical status | Falsifiable in ~4 yrs | Partly mathematical; possibly **undecidable empirically** | Different category |

**Where the PhD sits:** ~1 of the 3 primitives (adaptation), at the
**phenomenological/measurement layer** (not the formal-invariant layer), in **a few
substrates**. An early foothold on a real face — base camp, not the summit.

## The bridge (route to the summit)

1. **Scope 1:** prove adaptive capability has a measurable invariant structure across
   socio-technical domains. *(load-bearing foothold)*
2. **Scope 2:** pair the ladder with **one** formal invariant (FEP or AIT) — enter the
   *language* layer on the *adaptation* primitive.
3. **Scope 3:** extend across radically different substrates — test substrate-independence for real.
4. **Scope 4:** attempt the structure+information+adaptation fusion — **and be willing
   to prove it cannot be done.**

## First concrete step (cheap, honest, now)

**Position paper:** *"Is the maturity ladder a low-resolution shadow of a free-energy /
algorithmic-information invariant?"*
- Frames the 11-level scale as a possible phenomenological projection of a deeper invariant.
- Puts the program on the scope-4 route at the cost of weeks, not years.
- Stays explicitly a **conjecture** — earns the frontier framing without claiming it.

## Discipline (carried from the scope contract)

- Scope-4 is a **conjecture to be earned or refuted**, never assumed.
- Prove the small invariant (scope 1) first; it grants standing for the rest.
- A bounded/negative answer to the scope-4 question is a success, not a failure.
- Do **not** import scope-4 vocabulary into scope-1 claims (the v21+ degeneration trap).
