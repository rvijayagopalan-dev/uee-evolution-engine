# Vision & Scope — North Star ↔ Thesis Bridge

*Connects the long-horizon vision (a universal science of adaptation) to the
falsifiable PhD core — and fixes the discipline that keeps them from contaminating
each other. Read alongside [`research-horizons.md`](./research-horizons.md) (the
candidate areas) and [`00-proposal/`](./00-proposal/) (the thesis).*

---

## The vision (north star — scope 3)

> **Create a universal science of adaptation** that explains how systems survive,
> learn, evolve, and thrive across all scales of reality — establishing a
> transdisciplinary discipline integrating Systems Theory, Cybernetics, Complexity
> Science, Information Theory, Evolutionary Theory, Artificial Intelligence,
> Economics, Governance, and Civilization Studies.

**Grand challenge it serves:** *design adaptive systems capable of sustaining
humanity for 1,000+ years while continuously responding to technological,
ecological, economic, and societal change.*

This is a **field-building research program of decades**, not a thesis. It is a
motivating north star — never a single provable claim.

## The thesis (falsifiable core — scope 1)

> A **domain-invariant maturity meta-model of adaptive capability**, empirically
> shown to **transfer** across unrelated domains and to **predict** adaptation
> outcomes.

This is the first **provable brick** of the vision: it contributes (1) a shared
*measurement instrument* for adaptive capability, and (2) empirical evidence for
*invariance* — the hardest, most falsifiable sub-claim inside the grand vision.

> **Naming convention.** The thesis is titled *A Domain-Invariant Maturity Model of
> Adaptive Capability* — **"maturity," not "evolution,"** because it validates a
> measurement scale, not an evolutionary mechanism (and "Evolution" is the ladder's
> top *level*, L10). The word **"Evolution" is reserved for the programme / brand
> (Universal Evolution Engine, scope 2–4)** — never the scope-1 thesis title.

## Why they must stay separate

A PhD is assessed by examiners who must be satisfied the claim is **achieved** and
could have been **refuted**. "A universal science across all scales" and a
"1,000-year design" can be neither verified nor falsified in a viva. Framing the
thesis *as* the vision makes it unexaminable — the failure mode that sank classical
General Systems Theory and the project's own metaphysical (v21+) tiers.

> **The one rule:** the PhD makes *exactly one falsifiable claim* and never borrows
> the grandeur of the vision to dress itself up. Earning scope 1 is what grants the
> standing to lead scopes 2 and 3.

## The nine disciplines, mapped to the tiers

| Discipline | Role in the program | Tier (see research-horizons) |
|------------|---------------------|------------------------------|
| Systems Theory | Generalize the construct (boundaries, viability) | Tier 1 |
| Cybernetics | The sense→adapt control loop; requisite variety | Tier 1 |
| Complexity Science | Agents, emergence, fitness landscapes | Tier 1 |
| Information Theory | Candidate formal invariant (entropy, predictive info) | Tier 2 |
| Evolutionary Theory | Candidate formal invariant (variation–selection–retention) | Tier 2 |
| Artificial Intelligence | Substrate to test the engine (meta-learning, ALife, agents) | Tier 3 |
| Economics | Applied layer — adaptive allocation under change | Scope 3 (applied) |
| Governance | Applied layer — coordination & decision at scale | Scope 3 (applied) |
| Civilization Studies | Applied layer — the 1,000-year challenge | Scope 3 (applied) |

The integration of these into "a coherent discipline" is the scope-3 work. The
thesis touches only the **Tier 1–2 kernel**, instantiated and tested in a few
**Tier 3** substrates, and *cites* the applied layers as motivation.

## The bridge (how the brick seeds the field)

```
PhD (scope 1)              →  Research program (scope 2)      →  Vision (scope 3)
maturity meta-model,          ladder + ONE formal invariant      universal science of
validated + predictive        tested across substrates           adaptation; 1000-yr design
[provable in ~4 yrs]          [postdoc/lab, 5–10 yrs]            [field-building, decades]
```

## Scope contract (self-check before any claim)

- [ ] Is this claim **falsifiable** and examinable on a thesis timescale? → scope 1.
- [ ] Does it require **multiple substrates / a formal invariant**? → scope 2 (later).
- [ ] Is it **civilization-scale / 1,000-year / all-scales**? → scope 3 (north star, not a deliverable).
- [ ] Am I borrowing scope-3 grandeur to justify a scope-1 chapter? → **stop, re-scope.**

## Verdict

**On track as the first foundational step toward the vision — yes.**
**On track to *be* the universal science — no, and it must not try.**
The credibility of the whole program depends on proving the invariance brick first.
