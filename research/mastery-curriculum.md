# Mastery Curriculum & Literature Review — Topics to Deep-Dive

*What to master to execute this PhD, with core literature, organized by priority
tier mapped to scope. Tiers 1–3 are mandatory for the thesis; Tier 4 is for the
broader programme — do NOT drown in it during the PhD (scope discipline).*

## TIER 1 — Research craft (mandatory; this is what *executes* the thesis)

| Topic | Why | Key literature |
|-------|-----|----------------|
| **Systematic literature review** | Run Study 1; prove the gap | PRISMA 2020; Webster & Watson 2002 ("Analyzing the past…"); Kitchenham; Tranfield et al. 2003 |
| **Design Science Research (DSR)** | Maturity models are DSR artifacts | Hevner et al. 2004; Peffers et al. (DSRM); Gregor & Hevner 2013 |
| **Maturity-model methodology** | Build/validate the instrument | Becker et al.; de Bruin & Rosemann; Mettler; Pöppelbuß & Röglinger |
| **Psychometrics & scale development** | Reliability + construct validity (S2) | Churchill 1979; DeVellis, *Scale Development*; Cohen's κ / Krippendorff's α |
| **Scaling models (orderability)** | Test P1 (Guttman-like ladder) | Guttman scaling; Mokken scale analysis |
| **Research design & validity** | Validity threats, quasi-experiments | Shadish, Cook & Campbell 2002 |
| **Statistics** | Regression/AUC (H2); factor analysis (P4) | Regression texts; EFA/CFA (e.g., Brown, *CFA for Applied Research*) |
| **Philosophy of science** | Framing + viva defense | Popper (falsifiability); Lakatos (research programmes); Kuhn |
| **Research ethics / IRB** | Mandatory for clinical + org surveys | Institutional IRB guidance; Belmont principles |

## TIER 2 — Theoretical substance (mandatory; the content of the framework)

| Topic | Why | Key literature |
|-------|-----|----------------|
| **DIKW hierarchy + its critiques** | Grounds levels L2–L5 | Ackoff 1989; Rowley 2007; Frické 2009 (critique — engage it) |
| **Cybernetics & systems theory** | Grounds the sense→adapt loop | Wiener, *Cybernetics*; Ashby, *Intro to Cybernetics*; von Bertalanffy; Beer (VSM) |
| **Complex adaptive systems** | Generalizes the construct | Holland 1975; Mitchell, *Complexity: A Guided Tour*; Miller & Page |
| **Organizational learning** | Grounds L8→L10 (single/double/deutero) | Argyris & Schön; Senge; Cohen & Levinthal (absorptive capacity) |
| **Dynamic capabilities & evolutionary economics** | Theory of L9–L10 | Teece 1997; Eisenhardt & Martin; Nelson & Winter 1982 |
| **Resilience & panarchy** | Adaptive-cycle analog | Holling; Gunderson & Holling, *Panarchy*; Walker et al. |

## Suggested sequence

1. **Tier 1 first** (research craft) — you cannot execute *anything* without DSR +
   SLR + psychometrics. Start here even before admission.
2. **Tier 2 in parallel** — read while drafting the theory chapter; engage the DIKW
   critiques head-on.
3. **Tier 3 as studies approach** — just-in-time domain familiarity per study.
4. **Tier 4 only after scope-1 results** — one position paper's worth (FEP *or* AIT),
   not a second PhD. Over-investing here is the scope-4 trap.
