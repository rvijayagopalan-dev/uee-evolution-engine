# Research Horizons — Toward a General Theory of Adaptive Systems

*Candidate research areas for evolving the maturity meta-model
([`00-proposal/`](./00-proposal/)) into a general theory of adaptive systems and,
as a north star, a "Universal Evolution Engine."*

> **The framing that matters.** A genuine *general theory* needs a **formal
> invariant** — something conserved or optimized that holds across substrates
> (farms, firms, hospitals, organisms, AIs). Without an invariant, "universal
> evolution engine" stays a metaphor. The candidate areas below do three distinct
> jobs: **generalize the construct**, **find the invariant**, and **test across
> substrates**. Keep them — and their claims — separate.

---

## Tier 1 — Direct generalizations (defensible, adjacent)

| Area | Bridge to this work | Candor |
|------|--------------------|--------|
| Complex Adaptive Systems (Holland; Santa Fe) | The 11 levels = a maturity ordering of CAS capabilities | Strong, well-trodden |
| Cybernetics & Viable System Model (Ashby; Beer) | Requisite variety + recursive viability = the sense→adapt loop; a rival to formalize against | Strong |
| Dynamic capabilities & evolutionary economics (Teece; Nelson & Winter) | Already the theory of L9–L10; generalizes "maturity" to selection of routines | Strong |
| Resilience & Panarchy (Holling) | The adaptive cycle (growth→conservation→release→reorganization) is a structural analog of the ladder | Strong, under-exploited |

## Tier 2 — Unifying-spine candidates (where a general theory lives)

Each offers a **formal invariant** that could make "universal" rigorous. Pick one as
the mathematical backbone beneath the phenomenological ladder.

| Candidate spine | Invariant offered | Fit / risk |
|-----------------|-------------------|------------|
| Free Energy Principle / Active Inference (Friston) | Adaptation = minimizing variational free energy | Highest upside; math-heavy; contested scope |
| Universal / Generalized Darwinism (Hodgson & Knudsen) | Variation–Selection–Retention as substrate-neutral algorithm | Clean, "universal" by design; may be too abstract to predict |
| Nonequilibrium thermodynamics / dissipative adaptation (Prigogine; England) | Adaptation as entropy export / dissipation-driven order | Deep physical grounding; hard to operationalize for orgs |
| Assembly Theory (Cronin & Walker) | A measurable "assembly index" detecting selection | Novel, measurable; young and debated |
| Computational mechanics (Crutchfield) | ε-machines; statistical complexity as maturity-of-structure | Rigorous data→knowledge bridge; niche |

> **Strategic move:** the 11-level ladder = the *phenomenological scale*; one Tier-2
> mechanism = the *generative invariant* beneath it. That pairing —
> **measurable maturity scale ↔ formal adaptive invariant** — is what would
> constitute a general theory of adaptive systems.

## Tier 3 — Substrate instantiations (where to test universality)

Universality is earned by surviving disconfirmation across unlike substrates:

- **Artificial Life & Open-Ended Evolution** — testbed for "infinite generativity";
  simulate whether the engine keeps producing novelty.
- **Multi-agent systems / collective intelligence** — substrate for the v41
  "collective intelligence / conscious evolution" framing.
- **Meta-learning / transfer learning / AGI** — "learning to learn" = L8→L10 in machines.
- **Social-ecological systems** — panarchy's home turf; high-stakes real-world test.
- **Major evolutionary transitions (Maynard Smith & Szathmáry)** — the original
  evolution engine; parallels the level jumps.

## Tier 4 — Formal & moonshot framing

- **Category theory / compositional systems theory** — a substrate-independent
  *algebra* of adaptive systems; functorial maps between domains = a rigorous version
  of "domain invariance." High risk, high prestige.
- **General Systems Theory 2.0** — position as the *falsifiable* successor to von
  Bertalanffy's never-empirical original. Good narrative, dangerous if taken literally.

## The three concentric scopes (be explicit which you claim)

| Scope | Horizon | Claim | Verdict |
|-------|---------|-------|---------|
| **1. PhD** | now–4 yr | Domain-invariant maturity meta-model, validated & predictive | **Do this** — achievable, defensible |
| **2. Research program** | 5–10 yr | Ladder + one Tier-2 invariant, tested across Tier-3 substrates | The actual "general theory of adaptive systems" |
| **3. Universal Evolution Engine** | north star | The integrating vision | Banner that motivates 1 & 2 — **not** a single provable claim |

**The discipline that keeps it credible:** let each scope make its **own falsifiable
claim**, and never borrow the grandeur of scope 3 to dress up scope 1. The failure
mode of this entire space — and what sank classical GST and the v21+ metaphysical
tiers — is collapsing all three scopes into one grand assertion.

## Suggested sequence

1. Finish scope 1 (the PhD) — earns the right to the rest.
2. In parallel, pick **one** Tier-2 invariant and write a position paper pairing it
   with the ladder (low cost, high signal).
3. Stand up **one** Tier-3 simulation (ALife / multi-agent) to test open-endedness
   cheaply before committing to scope 2.
